Electrode–skin impedance imbalance measured in the frequency domain

Created2023-10-22|Updated2023-10-22|ReadPaper
|Post Views:

第一作者：Benjamin C. Fortune;Christopher G. Pretty;Chris J. Cameron;Lachlan R. McKenzie;Logan T. Chatfield;Michael P. Hayes

引用格式：[1]Fortune C B ,Pretty G C ,Cameron J C , et al.Electrode–skin impedance imbalance measured in the frequency domain[J].Biomedical Signal Processing and Control,2021,63

链接：

Electrode–skin impedance imbalance measured in the frequency domain.pdf

摘要

Objective: The objective of this study was to measure the magnitude and phase imbalance of the electrode–skin interface using silver/silver chloride (Ag/AgCl) and silver (Ag) electrode pairs. The study highlights the importance of balancing both the magnitude and phase of the electrode–skin interface.Methods: The study recorded the electrode–skin impedance imbalance in ten healthy subjects over a frequency range of 1 Hz–100 kHz. Three electrode configurations were used: Ag/AgCl electrodes without skin preparation (Ag/AgClNSP), Ag/AgCl electrodes with skin preparation (Ag/AgClSP), and Ag electrodes with skin preparation (Ag-SP). A compensation network was developed to simulate impedance imbalance reduction based on the experimental data.Results: At 50 Hz, the mean electrode–skin impedance imbalance was as follows:- Ag/AgClNSP electrode configuration: (37.6 ± 47.1) k𝛺 and (15.0 ± 18.3) degrees.- Ag/AgClSP electrode configuration: (4.52 ± 7.65) k𝛺 and (4.6 ± 6.9) degrees.- Ag-SP electrode configuration: (36.2 ± 45.1) k𝛺 and (3.4 ± 3.6) degrees.The compensation network reduced the mean impedance imbalance over the bioelectrical signal range (1 Hz–500 Hz) as follows:- Ag/AgClNSP: 284.3 k𝛺 and 11.9 degrees.- Ag/AgClSP: 4.6 k𝛺 and 6.2 degrees.- Ag-SP: 86.7 k𝛺 and 2.5 degrees.Conclusion: The study confirmed that electrode–skin impedance imbalance can be substantial and varies among subjects. While abrasive skin preparation reduces this imbalance, it does not guarantee a balanced electrode–skin interface. Therefore, using a compensation network to balance the electrode–skin impedance has the potential to reduce interference in bioelectrical signal recordings.Significance: Given that bioelectrical signal interference is influenced by electrode–skin impedance imbalance, the addition of compensatory impedance is necessary to improve the noise immunity of bioelectrical signal recordings and achieve a balanced electrode–skin interface.

内容记录

团队：新西兰基督城坎特伯雷大学生物工程中心，新西兰基督城坎特伯雷大学电气与计算机工程系

问题：测量银/氯化银（Ag/AgCl）和银（Ag）电极对的电极-皮肤界面的大小和相位失衡

方法：在十名健康受试者的皮肤上放置两个电极，记录了电极-皮肤阻抗失衡在1 Hz至100 kHz的频率范围内，使用三种电极配置：未经皮肤准备的Ag/AgCl电极（Ag/AgClNSP）、经过皮肤准备的Ag/AgCl电极（Ag/AgClSP）以及经过皮肤准备的Ag电极（Ag-SP）。开发了一种补偿网络，用实验数据模拟了阻抗失衡的减小。

效果：补偿网络导致了在生物电信号范围（1 Hz–500 Hz）内的电极-皮肤阻抗失衡平均减小

不足：本研究证实了电极-皮肤阻抗失衡可能很大，且在受试者之间存在差异。尽管磨擦皮肤准备可以减小电极-皮肤阻抗失衡，但不能保证一个平衡的电极-皮肤界面。因此，使用补偿网络平衡电极-皮肤阻抗有潜力减少生物电信号的干扰。

值得学习的地方

  • 该研究使用非线性最小二乘（NLLS）回归方法来设计并实现补偿网络，以平衡电极皮肤界面的阻抗。

  • 研究通过最小化实验中电极皮肤阻抗数据的幅度和相失衡来模拟电极皮肤阻抗。
